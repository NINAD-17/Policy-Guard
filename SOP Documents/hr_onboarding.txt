1. PURPOSE & OBJECTIVE
   This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the mandatory sequence of actions
   required during employee onboarding. The objective is to verify candidate identity,
   ensure agreement to confidentiality terms, provide necessary hardware, and restrict
   IT systems access until identity checks are formally verified, ensuring 100% security
   compliance.

2. SCOPE
   This procedure governs the onboarding lifecycle of all new hires, contractors,
   temporary consultants, and interns joining PulseGuard Technologies Inc. It dictates
   all coordination tasks between HR, IT, and Hiring Managers.

3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
   3.1. HR Coordinator
        - Inspects new hire identity documents.
        - Verifies Compliance and Confidentiality Agreement signatures.
        - Updates the HR system status to "Verified".
   3.2. IT Support Technician
        - Provisions laptop/hardware assets.
        - Creates internal email and VPN access accounts.
        - Holds access details until HR issues the clearance notification.
   3.3. Hiring Manager
        - Defines departmental training and onboarding plans.
        - Assigns onboarding buddies.

4. DEFINITIONS AND REFERENCES
   - Identity Verification: The physical or video validation of two government-issued 
     documents to confirm candidate identity.
   - NDA: Non-Disclosure Agreement / Compliance and Confidentiality Agreement.
   - SOP-HR-001: Remote Work Expectations and Procedures (reference document).

5. MANDATORY ONBOARDING COMPLIANCE SEQUENCE
   The onboarding sequence must follow these strict timelines:
   5.1. Identity Verification Timing
        - The HR coordinator must verify two government-issued IDs (e.g. Passport,
          Driver's License, or SSN card) on or before the employee's first working day.
   5.2. Agreement Execution Timeframe
        - The new hire must sign the formal Compliance and Confidentiality Agreement
          within 3 business days of onboarding.
   5.3. IT Account Activation Constraint
        - IT system access accounts (VPN, Github, email, Slack) must not be activated 
          until the identity verification step is marked complete in the HR database.
        - Early activation of accounts prior to ID verification is a severe audit fail.
   5.4. Equipment Delivery Timing
        - All physical IT hardware (laptops, security keys) must be shipped to 
          arrive at the employee's registered address at least 5 days prior to their 
          start date.

6. STEP-BY-STEP WORKFLOW
   Step 1: HR extends formal offer. Candidate accepts and signs offer letter.
   Step 2: HR coordinator requests shipping address and sends to IT.
   Step 3: IT ships pre-configured laptop to arrive 5 days before start date.
   Step 4: New Hire submits high-resolution copies of two government IDs.
   Step 5: HR coordinator conducts video call verification of IDs on day 1.
   Step 6: HR coordinator updates status to "ID Verified" in system.
   Step 7: IT Support Technician receives status alert and activates active directory.
   Step 8: New Hire logs in, reviews compliance docs, and signs the NDA within 3 days.
   Step 9: Onboarding checklist is archived in the employee's HR file.

7. EXCEPTION AND ESCALATION HANDLING
   7.1. Missing Documentation
        - If an employee fails to sign the Compliance Agreement in 3 days, their accounts
          must be suspended, and the case escalated to the HR Director.
   7.2. Escalation Contact Path
        - First Level: HR Coordinator.
        - Second Level: HR Director (Kavita Sharma).
        - Executive Escalation: VP of Engineering (Sneha Deshmukh).
